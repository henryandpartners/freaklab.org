]]> https://astrobio.freaklab.org/class-3/feed/ 14 Final Project Announcement https://astrobio.freaklab.org/final-project/ https://astrobio.freaklab.org/final-project/#respond Tue, 27 Aug 2019 19:16:56 +0000 http://astrobio.freaklab.org/?p=173 BIT 667 : AstroBiotechnology class is a graduate-level class, students are expected to participate in hands-on space biotechnology research. Students must work in a team (maximum of 2 persons/team) to develop provocative ideas and tangible prototypes that show how biotechnology could be used in space environment. Students would have access to the research facility of the FREAK Lab located on the sixth floor of the Bio-process innovation building (BRI), KMUTT. Please contact Professor Werasak for any questions : werasak@pdti.kmutt.ac.th

Key dates

  • 2 Progress Presentation (Week 4, 8)
  • Proposal Presentation (Week 12)
  • Final Presentation (Week 16)
  • Tangible Prototype (Week 16)

Sources for Inspiration

  • https://www.nasa.gov/mission_pages/station/research/experiments/explorer/
  • https://biodesignchallenge.org/
  • https://www.media.mit.edu/groups/space-exploration/projects/

Deliverables

#1 Progress Presentation

  • 2 slide per person
  • 5 minute per person
  • Upload your slide to google slide by Sunday (The link is already on the website under week 4)
  • Find 3 craziest/coolest/most innovative/most inspiring research projects that are related to Space Biotechnology
  • Come up with 3 craziest/coolest/most innovative/most inspiring research projects that are related to Space Biotechnology

#2 Progress Presentation

  • 2 slide per group
  • 10 minute per group 
  • Upload your slide to google slide by Sunday (The link is already on the website under week 8)
  • Find 3 craziest/coolest/most innovative/most inspiring research projects that are related to Space Biotechnology
  • Come up with 3 craziest/coolest/most innovative/most inspiring research projects that are related to Space Biotechnology

#3 Proposal Presentation

  • Max 10 slide per group
  • 10 minute per group 
  • Pick the coolest project that could become a prototype/research.
  • Present the idea, motivation, background & related work, project plan, project timeline, budget

#4 Final Presentation (in class)

  • 10 minute per group of your provocative ideas
  • Demonstration of the tangible prototypes

#5 Final Presentation (in public)

  • 10 minute per group of your provocative ideas
  • Exhibition of the tangible prototypes
